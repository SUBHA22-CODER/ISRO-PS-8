Bhatt, M., Chauhan, P., Sarkar, S., & Mohan, S. (2024)

Title: Evidence of subsurface water-ice in doubly shadowed craters of the
       lunar south polar region from Chandrayaan-2 DFSAR data

Published in: Icarus, Volume 408, Article 115825

Key Findings:
- Analysis of fully polarimetric DFSAR data reveals radar signatures consistent
  with subsurface water ice in four doubly shadowed craters within PSRs.
- Refined detection criterion: CPR > 1.0 AND DOP < 0.13 applied simultaneously
  distinguishes volumetric scattering (ice) from surface scattering (rocks).
- Strongest evidence found in a 1.1 km diameter crater within the Faustini
  permanently shadowed region, which also displays lobate rim morphology
  consistent with impact into ice-rich subsurface material.
- Doubly shadowed craters maintain temperatures ~25 K (-248°C) — among the
  coldest environments in the solar system — ideal for preserving ancient
  volatile deposits over geological timescales.
